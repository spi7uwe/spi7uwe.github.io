This project exists on unceded Secwepemc territory. Language, land, and identity are inseparable.

Secwepemctsném kt wel me7 yews.

We speak Secwepemctsín forever.

What this is

This is a Secwepemctsín language space: for speaking, listening, learning, and carrying the language forward in real life.

The goal is not “content.” The goal is use: words in motion, voices in context, and language that stays connected to land, family, and responsibility.

Language-first

Secwepemctsín is not a novelty and not a museum piece. It is a living language. This site puts the language first, and English second.

If you hear mixed phrasing, rhythm, or unusual segments in recordings, that is part of spoken expression. The language remains Secwepemctsín.

Awakening through language

Language carries memory. It carries teachings, humour, relationship, and law. Speaking it is a way of awakening who we are — not as an idea, but as practice.

This work is grounded in lived use: lessons, phrases, stories, and recordings that grow over time.

What this is not

This is not branding, not institutional ownership, and not permission-based identity. It is speaker-led and community-rooted.

Living and unfinished

This site will evolve. There will be corrections. Some things will be refined. That is normal for living language work.

Perfection freezes language. Use keeps it alive.
